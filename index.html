Persevere Annual Performance Review & Self-Evaluation









Annual Performance Review is a conversation between a Supervisor and an Employee about the Employee's performance. The Annual Performance Review will help establish timelines, goals, and resource allocation with which to accomplish the established employee's developmental goals and supervisor position expectations throughout the year.



The Annual Performance review highlights the following areas of an Employees overall performance:
  1. Essential Job Functions/Responsibilities: Select two or three essential job functions/responsibilities for the evaluation of an Employee's job Knowledge.
  2. Organizational Goals: An Assigned organization goal will be given annually to all Employees.
  3. Departmental Goals: Select one (1) departmental/unit goal (assigned to every employee in the department/unit).
  4. Employee Development Goals: Select two to three individual Employee development goals for the next twelve (12) consecutive months.
  5. Performance Factors: Employee Performance Factor(s) annual ratings

Assigned goals should be objective and subjective, using SMART Goals that are Specific, Measurable, Attainable, Result-oriented, & Time-specific. With the use of SMART Goals, Employee goals must be aligned with one of the performance factors outlined below:

Performance Factors
  • Quality: The extent of how thorough, neat and accurate the Employee's work is.
  • Productivity: The extent to which an Employee produces a significant amount of work efficiently and in a specified time period.
  • Job Knowledge: The extent to which an Employee possesses the practical/technical knowledge, and ability to apply the skills required for the job.
  • Initiative:The extent to which an Employee seeks out new assignments and assumes responsibility for additional duties when necessary
  • Interpersonal Relationships: The extent to which an Employee willingly demonstrates the ability to cooperate, work and communicate with his/her co-workers, supervisors, subordinates and/or outside contacts.
  • Judgement:The extent to which an Employee demonstrates proper judgement and decision making skills when necessary.

Use the following scale to asses Employee performance:
  1. Does Not Meet Expectations- Performance Is consistently below expectations in areas of responsibility. Employee performs assigned responsibilities in an unsatisfactory manner, has not responded to constructive feedback or has not improved his/her performance.
  2. Meets Some Expectations- Employee performs satisfactory in some aspects of his/her assigned responsibilities, but not in others. Performance may be marginal or inconsistent. Improvements are required to meet expectations.
  3. Meets Expectations- Usually meets performance expectations in a satisfactory manor.
  4. Exceeds Expectations- Consistently meets, and often exceeds performance expectations.
  5. Significantly Exceeds Expectations- Performs assigned responsibilities in a consistently superior manner, as demonstrated through the throughout the review period; Always exceeds expectations, and is recognized as an expert in this field.
